# Deserted Tales Lore

A living archive of Moroccan history, weaving together the real events of the Maghreb's most fascinating dynasties with rich speculative narrative.

## What Is This?

**Deserted Tales Lore** is a comprehensive worldbuilding project exploring Morocco's historical past—particularly the underappreciated Berber kingdoms and dynasties that shaped the region. Think of it as a love letter to Moroccan history, told through detailed character profiles, realm documents, and interconnected lore.

The project currently focuses on the **Barghawata Confederacy** (744–1058 CE)—a remarkable 314-year Berber independent kingdom along Morocco's Atlantic coast that developed its own unique syncretic faith, including a Berber-language Quran.

### The Story

The Barghawata weren't just another medieval kingdom. They emerged from the Great Berber Revolt (739–743 CE), created a religion that blended Islam with ancient Berber traditions, developed their own scripture, and held off the Umayyads, Zirids, and eventually the Almoravids for over three centuries.

Their story includes:
- A prophetic claim and an 80-surah Berber Quran
- A policy of religious concealment (taqiyyah) for generations
- A messianic prophecy that the prophet would return
- Diplomatic relations with the Umayyad Caliphate of Córdoba
- Warriors who fought across North Africa
- And ultimately, a fall to the Almoravids that ended a unique experiment in Berber self-determination

## The Mystery at the Heart of the Barghawata

One of the most intriguing aspects of this history is the debate over whether Salih ibn Tarif ever actually claimed prophethood—or whether his grandson Yunus invented the entire religious system decades later to legitimize his rule. The project documents both the traditional narrative and the historical reality, leaving room for interpretation.
