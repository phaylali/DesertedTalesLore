## Biography
Dawud al-Rundi al-Nafzi was a Berber leader who briefly ruled the Kingdom of Nekor in 712 CE. Dissatisfied with the strict Islamic restrictions imposed by Emir Salih I ibn Mansur, the local Berber tribes deposed Salih and installed Dawud as ruler. However, his reign was short-lived, as the tribes soon grew dissatisfied with him as well and reinstated Salih I.

## Skills and Abilities
Dawud possessed strong tribal influence and leadership among the Nafzi Berbers, enabling him to mobilize support against the ruling emir. His actions reflected the tensions between Arab-influenced Islam and traditional Berber customs.
