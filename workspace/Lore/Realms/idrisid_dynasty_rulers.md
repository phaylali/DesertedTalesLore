# Idrissid Dynasty — Complete Ruler List

## Overview

The **Idrissid dynasty** (788–985 CE / 172–375 AH) was the first independent Islamic state in Morocco and the first Sharifian dynasty in the western Maghreb. Founded by **Idris ibn Abdallah**, a descendant of the Prophet Muhammad through Ali and Hasan, the dynasty ruled from Fez and shaped the early Islamization and Arabization of Morocco. The dynasty ended when the last ruler, **Al-Hasan ibn al-Qasim**, was killed by Umayyad forces in 985 CE.

### B. Interregnum: Miknasa Control (919/921–925)

Musa ibn Abi al-Afiyah, leader of the Miknasa Berbers, controlled Fez and Idrissid territories on behalf of the Fatimid Caliphate during this period.

## Dynasty Phases

| Phase | Period (CE) | Duration | Description |
|-------|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Foundation** | 788–828 | ~40 years | Idris I and Idris II establish and consolidate the state |
| **Strength** | 828–863 | ~35 years | Muhammad, Ali I, and Yahya I maintain relative stability |
| **Fragmentation** | 863–927 | ~64 years | Repeated successions, Kharijite revolts, Fatimid intervention, loss of Fez |
| **Northern Remnant** | 927–985 | ~58 years | Reduced Idrissid state in the Rif, caught between Fatimids and Umayyads |

## Territorial Division of 828 CE

Upon the death of Idris II, his son Muhammad divided the kingdom among his brothers, creating eight semi-autonomous statelets. Muhammad retained Fez with only nominal authority.

| Ruler | Territory | Region |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Muhammad ibn Idris II** | Fez (capital) | Central |
| **Al-Qasim ibn Idris II** | Tangier, Ceuta, Tetouan, Hajar al-Nisr | North |
| **Umar ibn Idris II** | Sanhaja, Ghumara, Rif, Taza | Northeast |
| **Isa ibn Idris II** | Chellah, Salé, Azemmour, Tamesna | Atlantic coast |
| **Yahya ibn Idris II** | Hiṣn Daī (near Beni Mellal) | Central-south |
| **Hamza ibn Idris II** | Walili (Volubilis) | Central |
| **Abdallah ibn Idris II** | South: Lamta territory, Tamdult | South |
| **Ahmad ibn Idris II** | Meknes, Fazaz, Tadla | Central-west |

*Note: Exact dates of rule for provincial governors are largely unknown.*
