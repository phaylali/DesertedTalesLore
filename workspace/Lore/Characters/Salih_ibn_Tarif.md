# Salih ibn Tarif

## Overview
**Prophet-King of the Barghawata** (r. ~744–792 CE), self-proclaimed **Mahdi** and author of a unique Berber-language "Quran" that fused Islam with indigenous Amazigh traditions. Transformed his father's tribal confederation into a theocratic state that endured 300+ years. Viewed by orthodox Islam as a heresiarch; viewed by modern Amazigh historians as a cultural resistance leader.

## The Birth Year Problem

**Critical Gap:** We know Salih died at age 47. But when?

### **Option A: Death ~779 CE**
- If prophet declaration was **744** and he ruled ~35 years
- **Birth:** ~732 CE
- **Problem:** Makes him only ~12 at accession—impossible

### **Option B: Death ~792 CE (Traditional)**
- Reigned ~48 years from 744
- **Birth:** ~745 CE
- **Problem:** This makes him born AFTER his father Tarif died (~744)! Chronologically impossible

### **Option C: Extended Timeline**
- **Birth:** ~715-720 CE
- **Prophet declaration:** ~749 CE (not 744)
- **Death:** ~766-767 CE at age 47
- **Coherent but contradicts** "ruled from 744" in some sources

### **Option D: Symbolic Age**
- "47" is numerologically significant (7×7 minus 2; or lunar year calculation)
- Actual age unknown; 47 is theological statement

**🎮 GAME DECISION:** For RPG purposes, **Option C** is cleanest if you want coherent biography: **Born ~715, prophet ~750, dies ~767** allows father Tarif to actually exist when Salih was born. Alternative: embrace contradiction—Salih's age is **mystical, not literal**.

## Ethno-Religious Claims

**Revolutionary Assertion:** Salih claimed his name was the **same prophet** referenced in multiple traditions:

| Language | Name | Meaning |
|----------|------|---------|
| **Arabic** | Ṣāliḥ (صالح) | "Righteous/virtuous" |
| **Syriac** | Mālik | "King/owner" |
| **Hebrew** | Rūbyā | Possible: "fourth" or prophetic title |
| **Berber** | Werba | "He has no successor" / ultimate prophet |

**Claim:** He was the **final prophet, superseding Muhammad for the Berbers**, while acknowledging Muhammad was indeed prophet—but **only for the Arabs**.

**🎮 RPG IMPLICATION:** This creates **massive political tension**. Salih's followers believe in ONE God (monotheism), accept Jesus as important, accept Muhammad as real prophet—but claim Salih is the **Mahdi** who brings correction/clarification. This is heresy to orthodox Muslims, but monotheism to Berber nationalists.

## Baraka: The Blessed Saliva

**Unique supernatural element:** Barghawata believed Salih and his descendants possessed **baraka (divine blessing)** transmitted through **saliva**.

### **Documented Practices**
- **Spitting for blessings:** Religious leader spits on supplicant as healing/blessing
- **Family transmission:** Only Salih's lineage carried authentic baraka
- **Political legitimacy:** Dynastic succession required patrilineal descent

**🎮 GAME MECHANIC:** Could be literal holy water mechanic (healing), reputation system (kissed by the blessed), or political legitimacy quest (prove descent).

## Historical Narrative

### **Early Life**
- Son of Tarif al-Matghari (founder)
- Raised in newly-independent Barghawata confederation (~744)
- Witness to tribal resistance against Umayyad reconquest

### **Prophetic Declaration (c. 744-749)**
- Claimed divine revelation
- Authored Berber-language "Quran"
- Declared himself **Mahdi** (messianic leader)
- Proclaimed **finality of his prophecy** for Berber peoples
- Acknowledged Muhammad as prophet for Arabs

### **Reign (~744/749–792)
- Established theocratic governance
- Converted 12 of 29 tribal confederation member tribes
- Maintained pragmatic tolerance toward remaining 17 orthodox/Sufri tribes
- Defended against Idrisid and Umayyad incursions
- **Disappeared/died at age 47** with promise of return

## Contradictions & Controversies

### **Historical Source Conflict**

| Source | View of Salih |
|--------|---------------|
| **Ibn Khaldun** (14th c.) | "Devout heretic"—acknowledges piety but condemns innovation |
| **al-Bakri** (11th c. geographer) | Polemical; emphasizes "witchcraft" and "paganism" |
| **Ibn Hazm** (11th c. jurist) | Explicit fatwa declaring Salih false prophet |
| **Modern Amazigh historians** | Cultural resistance leader; indigenous rights champion |
| **Skeptical historians** | Opportunist who used religion to consolidate power |

### **Critical Question: Did Salih invent the religion, or formalized existing practices?**

**Arabic sources imply:** Salih CREATED innovation single-handedly

**Alternative reading:** Tarif or earlier Berber Muslims had already synthesized practices; Salih CODIFIED and LEGITIMIZED them through prophetic claim

**🎮 RPG POTENTIAL:** Let players discover pre-744 Barghawata sites that suggest syncretism PREDATES Salih—hinting he was the **prophet, not the architect**.

## Legacy

### **Direct Impact**
- Established 314-year dynasty (744–1058)
- Created model for Berber Islamic autonomy
- Preserved Tamazight language in religious context
- Demonstrated viability of non-Arab Islamic alternative

### **Modern Significance**
- **Marginalized in Moroccan curricula:** Often omitted or condemned as heresy
- **Amazigh identity symbol:** Reclaimed by indigenous rights movements as cultural resistance icon
- **"Devout Heretic":** Paradox—pious yet condemned; challenges binary of orthodox/heretic
