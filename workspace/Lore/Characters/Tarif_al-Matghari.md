# Tarif al-Matghari

## Overview
Founder of the **Barghawata Confederacy** (744 CE), a Masmuda Berber polity that endured over 300 years on Morocco's Atlantic coast, defying Umayyad, Idrisid, Fatimid, and finally Almoravid power.

## Place of Birth: Contradictions & Options

**Source Conflict:** Medieval Arabic chroniclers provide TWO competing origin stories:

### ~~**Option A: Iberian Origin (Andalusi Connection)**~~ ❌ REJECTED
~~The name "Barghawata" possibly derives from "**Barbati**" — a nickname indicating origins near **Rio Barbate (ancient Barbat)** near Cádiz, Spain.~~
- **Why rejected:** In-game legend/propaganda only; historically false for this lore
- **In-universe use:** NPCs may *believe* this or tell the romantic story, but it's not true

### **Option B: Indigenous Moroccan Origin**
**Jérôme Carcopino** (historian) argues "Barghawata" descends from ancient **Baquates** tribe near Volubilis, mentioned in Roman sources (pre-7th century).
- **Evidence:** Older Berber tribal continuity; no need for Iberian connection
- **Supporters:** Modern Amazigh nationalist historians
- **Narrative Appeal:** Deep indigenous roots, resistance to Arab-centric framing

### **✅ CANON: Indigenous Moroccan Origin (Selected)**
**Decision:** Tarif was indigenous to the Tamesna region. The "Barbati" etymology is an **in-universe legend** or **Arab chronicler confusion**—useful for NPC dialogue about foreign origins, but not historically true.

**Grounded Theory:** The name "Barghawata" descends from ancient **Baquates** tribe mentioned in Roman sources (near Volubilis). Tarif's people were **Masmuda Berbers** indigenous to Atlantic Morocco for centuries before Islam.

**Implications:**
- No Iberian sojourn; Tarif never left Morocco
- "Barbati" story is propaganda (maybe spread by later rivals?) or honest confusion by outsiders
- Connection to land is ancestral, not acquired
- **Most plausible for realistic RPG:** Indigenous origin with possible Andalusi contact/experience

**🎮 GAME IMPLEMENTATION:**
- **Canonical truth:** Indigenous origin (Option B)
- **In-world legends:** Some NPCs (especially Andalusi traders/Arab chroniclers) tell the "Barbati from Spain" story
- **Player discovery:** Can uncover evidence that Baquates lineage predates Tarif by centuries—ancestral land claim vs. foreign adventurer narrative

## Historical Role

### **Military & Political Career**

1. **Revolt Participant (740-743):** Joined the Great Berber Revolt against Umayyad taxation and ethnic hierarchy, fighting alongside Ghomara, Miknasa, and Zenata tribes under Kharijite banners.

2. **Separatist Strategist (742-744):** Recognized the revolt's fragmentation and Umayyad counter-offensives. With the primary goal (liberation from Arab direct rule) achieved, he prioritized **tribal preservation** over ideological purity.

3. **State Founder (744):** Established autonomous confederation in **Tamesna region** (Safi–Salé coastal plain), uniting ~29 tribes: 12 adopting future Barghawata faith, 17 remaining orthodox/Sufri.

### **Governance Philosophy (Inferred)**
- **Pragmatism over dogma:** Abandoned failing Kharijite revolt to build sustainable autonomy
- **Tribal federalism:** Balanced religious innovation (later expanded by Salih) with pragmatic tolerance
- **Defensive focus:** Built fortified positions; leveraged guerrilla tactics and terrain

## Conflicting Narratives

### **The "Religious Founder" Question**

**Arabic Sources (Ibn Khaldun, al-Bakri):**
- Describe Tarif as political/military founder
- Credit **Salih ibn Tarif** with ALL religious innovation (prophetic claims, Berber Quran, syncretic faith)
- Tarif depicted as pragmatic chieftain, not prophet

**Alternative Interpretation (Some Modern Amazigh Historians):**
- Suggest Tarif laid **cultural groundwork** that Salih formalized
- Barghawata religion may have had deeper indigenous roots than Arabic sources admit

**🎮 RPG IMPLICATION:** In your game world, consider letting Tarif have **secret/unorthodox beliefs** that Salih later "revealed." Adds depth—Tarif as the subtle founder, Salih as the revolutionary prophet.
