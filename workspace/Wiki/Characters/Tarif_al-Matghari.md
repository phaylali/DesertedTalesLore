# Tarif al-Matghari

## Overview
Tarif al-Matghari (15 May 695 – c. 744) was a Masmuda Berber chieftain who founded the Barghawata tribal confederation in the Tamesna region of Atlantic Morocco. After participating in the Muslim conquest of Iberia at age sixteen, he established an autonomous political entity upon returning to Morocco, creating a state that endured for over three centuries until the Almoravid conquest.

## Origins & Lineage

### Birth & Early Life
Tarif was born into the **Matghara** subtribe of the Masmuda confederation on **15 May 695**, in the Tamesna coastal plains of Atlantic Morocco. His family were indigenous agriculturalists established in the region for centuries, tracing lineage to the **Baquates** people attested in Roman-era sources near Volubilis.

### The Iberian Expedition (711)
At age sixteen, Tarif participated in the Muslim crossing to Iberia in 711. During his time there:
- Acquired **Andalusi garments and textiles** that later led to the erroneous "Barbati" origin legend
- Observed Umayyad military organization and coastal fortification techniques
- Returned to Tamesna by 712–714 with practical knowledge of external political organization

**Canon Clarification:** The persistent claim that "Barghawata" derives from "Barbati" (Barbate, Spain) is erroneous. Tarif had no Iberian ancestry—the nickname arose from his distinctive dress acquired during the expedition, which foreign chroniclers later misinterpreted as ethnic origin. He remained culturally and ethnically **Matghari Masmuda**.

## Biography

### Early Years (695–711)
Raised in the agricultural settlements of the Tamesna plain, Tarif learned tribal governance through participation in the council systems (*jema'a*) of the Masmuda confederation. His youth coincided with the initial Arab-Berber military organization in North Africa.

### The Iberian Interlude (711–714)
Participating in the crossing at sixteen, Tarif served in logistics and scouting roles rather than frontline combat. His acquisition of Andalusi clothing and goods would later create the foundation of origin myths—the garments marking him visually distinct from other Berber warriors upon his return, interpreted by outsiders as Iberian heritage.

### The Great Berber Revolt (740–743)
Following Umayyad fiscal policies that discriminated against Berber converts, Tarif joined the revolt alongside his fellow Matghara under **Maysara al-Matghari** (tribal namesake, not relative). The rebellion successfully expelled Umayyad forces from Morocco, but fractured by 742 due to:
- Internal disputes between Zenata chieftains and Sufri commissars
- Divergent goals among tribal confederations

Recognizing the revolt's unsustainable trajectory, Tarif executed a strategic withdrawal to Tamesna in 742–743, prioritizing tribal preservation over continued insurgency.

### Founding the Confederacy (744)
Consolidating approximately **29 tribes** in the Tamesna region:
- **12 tribes** would later adopt the distinctive Barghawata faith (under Salih)
- **17 tribes** maintained Sufri Kharijite or orthodox alignments

Tarif established defensive positions and tribal councils, creating a decentralized political entity independent of Umayyad—and later Abbasid—authority. His governance emphasized:
- Agricultural self-sufficiency
- Tribal autonomy balanced with collective defense
- Pragmatic tolerance rather than ideological dogma

### Death & Succession (c. 744)
Tarif died in approximately 744 of natural causes (specific circumstances unrecorded). Leadership transferred immediately to his son **Salih ibn Tarif**, establishing the hereditary Barghawata dynasty.

**Critical Note:** Tarif died without knowledge of Salih's religious innovations. The foundational political structure was Tarif's work; the theological architecture became Salih's contribution.

## Political & Religious Orientation

### Governance Philosophy
- **Pragmatic Separatism:** Secured autonomy while avoiding provocation of Umayyad counter-offensives
- **Tribal Federalism:** Balanced centralized coordination with clan-level autonomy
- **Defensive Focus:** Prioritized agricultural fortification over territorial expansion

### Religious Position
During Tarif's lifetime, the confederation maintained loose association with **Sufri Kharijite** doctrine—the revolutionary Islamic sect that had fueled the 740 revolt. The Kharijite principle of **elective merit** (rulership not restricted to Quraysh/Sharifian lineage) provided ideological justification for independent Berber governance.

**Important:** While Tarif respected Kharijite egalitarianism, he established the political framework that enabled—without directing—his son's later religious developments.

## Legacy & Significance

### Historical Impact
- Established a **314-year** indigenous Berber state (744–1058)
- Created the political foundation enabling Salih's religious synthesis
- Demonstrated viability of autonomous Berber governance without Arab caliphal structures
- Provided model for later Almohad and Marinid state formation

### The "Barbati" Legend (Retrospective)
Later Arab chroniclers (al-Bakri, Ibn Khaldun) recorded the Iberian origin theory. Modern analysis suggests this derived from:
1. Tarif's Andalusi-acquired clothing distinguishing him visually
2. Chroniclers' difficulty comprehending indigenous Berber state formation
3. Etymological confusion between "Barghawata" and "Barbati"

**Canonical Resolution:** Tarif was indigenous to Tamesna; the legend reflects external misinterpretation, not fact.
