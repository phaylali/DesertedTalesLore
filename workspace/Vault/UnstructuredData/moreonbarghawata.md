## 🏛️ **RELIGIOUS DOCTRINE (As Revealed by Yunus)**

### **Core Beliefs**
- **God's Name**: **Yakûsh** (Berber for "the giver" or "the immense")
- **Prophets**: Muhammad (Arab prophet) + **Salih ibn Tarif** (Berber prophet)
- **Holy Book**: 80 suras in **Tamazight** (not Arabic)
- **Mahdi**: Salih ibn Tarif (awaited return)

### **Unique Practices**
| Practice | Orthodox Islam | Barghawata Religion |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Daily Prayers** | 5 | **10** (5 day + 5 night) |
| **Fasting Month** | Ramadan | **Rajab** |
| **Assembly Day** | Friday | **Thursday** |
| **Prayer Posture** | Standing + prostration | **Half standing, half sitting** |
| **Dietary Taboos** | Pork, alcohol | **Eggs, rooster meat, animal heads** |
| **Allowed Foods** | — | **Wild boar meat** |
| **Marriage Rules** | 4 wives max | **Unlimited wives** (no restrictions) |

### **Sacred Formulas (In Tamazight)**

- **Moqqar Yakûsh** = "God is great"
- **Our d am Yakûsh** = "There is no one like him"
- **Iddjen Yakûsh** = "God is one"

## ⚔️ **MILITARY & DIPLOMACY**

### **Military Strength**
- **3,200 knights** under Salih's direct command
- **10,000 horsemen** from allied Berber tribes
- **12,000 additional horsemen** from Muslim allies
- **Total**: ~25,000 mounted forces at peak

### **Diplomatic Strategy**
- **Abdellah Abu an-Ansar** (10th century): Would gather army, threaten neighbors, accept gifts, then disband army — creating fear without bloodshed
- **Relations with Umayyads**: Initially friendly; broke in late 10th century
- **Relations with Fatimids**: Hostile — repelled multiple invasions

### **Key Battles**
- **740**: Withdrew from Berber Revolt alliance
- **977–978, 998–999**: Repelled Umayyad invasions from Spain
- **982–983**: Repelled Fatimid attack
- **1058**: Killed **Abdallah ibn Yasin** (Almoravid spiritual leader) in battle
- **1058**: **Destroyed by Almoravids** — end of confederacy

2. **Masmouda**: Privileged social rank
3. **Zenata & Senhaja**: Enhanced status through commerce
4. **Sudanese-origin tribes**: Allies controlling Sahara caravan routes

## 🌟 **PARALLEL MOVEMENT: Ha-Mîm**

- **Who**: Another Berber "prophet" contemporary with later Barghawata period
- **When**: ~925 CE
- **Where**: Ghomara tribe near Tetouan (Western Rif)
- **Religion**: Composed his own Koran in Tamazight
- **Fate**: Killed in battle 928/931; sect survived until 11th century when Almohads forced conversion
- **Significance**: Shows Barghawata weren't unique — other Berber groups also created syncretic religions

## 🎯 **THE ULTIMATE PLOT TWIST FOR YOUR LORE**

### **The Revelation Arc**

**Act 1: The Official History**
- Player learns the "standard" story: Salih was a prophet who disappeared, promising to return during the 7th king's reign
- The Barghawata religion is presented as ancient and authentic

**Act 2: The Scholar's Discovery**
- Player finds evidence that Salih **never claimed prophethood publicly**

**Act 3: The Conspiracy Theory**
- Player uncovers that Yunus **invented the entire religion** and attributed it to his grandfather
- The "7th king prophecy" was a **political tool** to create messianic hope
- Salih's disappearance may have been **assassination or exile**, not divine ascension

**Act 4: The Truth**
- The Barghawata religion was a **revolutionary act of cultural resistance**
- Yunus used his grandfather's name to **legitimize Berber independence from Arab Islam**
- The "prophetic claims" were **symbolic** — asserting that Berbers could have their own religious authority
- The entire system was designed to **preserve Berber identity** against Arab-Islamic hegemony
