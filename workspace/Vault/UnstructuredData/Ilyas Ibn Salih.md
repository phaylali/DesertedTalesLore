# Ilyas Ibn Salih

## Core Identity
- **Name**: Ilyas ibn Salih
- **Title**: 2nd Ruler of Barghawata Confederacy
- **Reign**: 791–842 CE (51 years)
- **Ethnicity**: Masmouda Berber
- **Symbol**: 🌟 (Guardian of the secret)

## Key Details
- **Role**: Continued his father Salih's *taqiyyah* policy - kept religion secret from general population
- **Succession**: Inherited throne at age 47 when father disappeared
- **Reign Length**: 51 years - longest reign of early Barghawata rulers
- **Religious Practice**: Publicly practiced orthodox Islam while secretly following his father's teachings

## Historical Significance
> "Ilyas ibn Salih maintained the religious concealment strategy that preserved the Barghawata faith during the early years of the confederacy. His 51-year reign provided crucial stability for the nascent state."

## The Plot Twist
> "He was the bridge between his father's secret teachings and his son's public revelation. Without his careful concealment, the Barghawata religion might have been destroyed in its infancy."

## Legacy
- **Military**: Maintained 3,200 knights and 10,000 horsemen from allied tribes
- **Diplomacy**: Established trade routes with Spain, Aghmat, and Sijilmassa
- **Cultural Impact**: Preserved Berber identity during Arab Islamic hegemony
